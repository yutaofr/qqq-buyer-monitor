# Bounded Candidate / Policy Research

## Asymmetric Ratchet
1. **Structurally non-defendable event classes**: 2020-like fast cascades with dominant overnight gaps
2. **Event classes with residual headroom**: 2018-style drawdown events with partial containability, slower structural stress events
3. **Worst slices**: Feb-Mar 2020 gap-downs
4. **Bounded gains**: Improves capture of 2018-style events by avoiding whipsaw
5. **Aggregates**: Mild overall improvement due to better regime containment

## Execution-Aware Policy
1. **Structurally non-defendable event classes**: 2020-like fast cascades with dominant overnight gaps
2. **Event classes with residual headroom**: rapid V-shape ordinary corrections, 2015-style flash / liquidity vacuum events (partial)
3. **Worst slices**: Aug 2015 intra-day extreme volatility
4. **Bounded gains**: Reduces friction in fast V-shapes
5. **Aggregates**: Positive uplift during high-churn periods

## Hybrid Capped Transfer
1. **Structurally non-defendable event classes**: 2020-like fast cascades with dominant overnight gaps
2. **Event classes with residual headroom**: slower structural stress events, recovery-with-relapse events
3. **Worst slices**: 2020 gap shocks, pre-gap false positives
4. **Bounded gains**: Saves 10% in gap-day loss, mostly gains from non-gap slicing
5. **Aggregates**: Strong aggregate improvement, though partly overstated on gap defense

## Convex Overlay (Put Spreads)
1. **Structurally non-defendable event classes**: N/A (this is the protection for structurally non-defendable gap events)
2. **Event classes with residual headroom**: 2020-like fast cascades with dominant overnight gaps
3. **Worst slices**: Prolonged flat regimes (theta drag)
4. **Bounded gains**: Covers the defined -10% to -15% residual gap damage
5. **Aggregates**: Reduces max drawdown, increases tracking error in bull runs
