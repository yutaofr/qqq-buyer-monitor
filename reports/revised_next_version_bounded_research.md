# Revised Bounded Candidate / Policy Research

## retained_asymmetric_ratchet
1. Structurally non-defendable event classes: 2020-like fast cascades with dominant overnight gaps
2. Loss-weighted importance: high for 2018-style and slower structural stress because improvable-loss score is high
3. Worst-slice behavior: must be measured against recovery-with-relapse and high-vol threshold-flip slices
4. Bounded gains: entry/exit asymmetry can improve containable drawdown response without claiming gap survivability
5. Aggregate only last: aggregate uplift is secondary to worst-slice behavior

## retained_execution_aware_policy
1. Structurally non-defendable event classes: 2020-like fast cascades with dominant overnight gaps
2. Loss-weighted importance: high where next-open penalties and churn dominate but event remains partly containable
3. Worst-slice behavior: must report gap-adjusted drawdown and recovery miss, not only close-to-close TTD
4. Bounded gains: can reduce policy translation damage, not overnight non-tradability
5. Aggregate only last: aggregate only after event-class decomposition

## hybrid_capped_transfer
1. Structurally non-defendable event classes: 2020-like fast cascades with dominant overnight gaps
2. Loss-weighted importance: secondary because non-gap slices dominate its gain
3. Worst-slice behavior: gap-day loss reduction is small and recovery miss can offset part of the benefit
4. Bounded gains: candidate may smooth non-gap transitions and recovery-with-relapse churn
5. Aggregate only last: aggregate uplift must be decomposed into gap and non-gap slices

## discrete_gearbox
1. Structurally non-defendable event classes: 2020-like fast cascades with dominant overnight gaps
2. Loss-weighted importance: limited to policy-meaningful 2018-style and relapse windows
3. Worst-slice behavior: 2015 liquidity-vacuum and relapse windows show threshold flapping risk
4. Bounded gains: may be studied inside ambiguity bands; cannot become a main policy family yet
5. Aggregate only last: no pooled score optimization

## convex_overlay
1. Structurally non-defendable event classes: policy/model layers do not remove 2020-like core overnight gap risk
2. Loss-weighted importance: high for tail loss, low frequency, expensive carry
3. Worst-slice behavior: must isolate overnight gap and liquidity-vacuum jump residuals
4. Bounded gains: put spreads/collars are most aligned; outright puts and VIX calls carry heavy costs
5. Aggregate only last: judge by residual target improvement net of benign-period drag
